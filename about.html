Introduction

The English Language Assistant Program in France (also called TAPIF / ALVE) is a French government-sponsored exchange program that allows young people from partner countries to live and work in France as English Teaching Assistants for 7 months, with the possibility of renewal.

This guide is based on official program information, personal research, and common problems, mistakes, and questions from applicants.

Important: This program is a means to an end, not an end in itself — so preparation determines how successful your experience will be.

Program Overview

Role
English Teaching Assistant
Workload
12 hours / week
Duration
7 months (Oct – Apr)
Salary
€800 – €813 / month
School level
Primary or Secondary (1–3 schools)
Note: You do NOT need a teaching certificate or English teaching certificate, as long as you are a native English speaker and meet the requirements.

What You Can & Cannot Do

The guidelines of what can be asked of you as a Language Assistant should, in theory, remain the same across all academies. If you're ever uncertain whether something your school asks of you is permitted, check this guide first or confirm with your Rectorat. The example below comes from the academie of Lyon.

✓ You Can

  • Make the students talk as much as possible
  • Bring cultural insight
  • Co-teach in the same classroom
  • Be in charge of small groups in a separate (but close) classroom
  • Help with recordings
  • Participate in special events (Day of the Assistants, festivities)
  • Help with grading oral performances

✗ You Cannot

  • Work with BTS or CPGE students
  • Give grammar lessons
  • Grade papers or formal oral exams (DNB, bac)
  • Teach the whole class alone
  • Spend an entire session with just one student
