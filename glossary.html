Reference

Glossary

French terms and abbreviations you'll run into during your assistantship — from paperwork to housing to banking. Type below to search.

A
Abonnement
Subscription for a certain amount of time. For example, an "abonnement mensuel" is a monthly subscription.
Accuser
To acknowledge.
Un accusé de réception
Confirmation of receipt.
Un acompte
(Payment) advance. You will need to make une demande d'acompte at the beginning of your contract to receive your pay earlier after your first month of working.
Un acte de naissance
Birth certificate.
Un.e allocataire
Beneficiary (for CAF).
Arrête de Nomination
Also referred to as just your arrête. The work contract given to you by your academy. This is your work contract.
Assurance habitation
Mandatory home insurance in France, covering damage to your apartment and sometimes third-party liability.
Attestation de droits (CPAM)
The document provided by the assurance maladie that proves that you are covered by the French health insurance.
Attestation d'hérbergement
Proof of housing. Can be written by the person hosting you (if living with a family or individual without a lease), or your school if you're living there without a lease document.
Attestation sur l'honneur
A document used to legally attest to something. You can find plenty of examples of this online.
Une attestation
Certificate, confirmation.
Avis d'imposition
Tax document received once a year summarizing your income and taxes owed (or refund). Received after filing taxes for the first time.
B
Bail
Lease agreement.
Bulletin de Salarie / Fiche de Paie
Paycheck. May be accessible online at ensap.gouv.fr — see the Paycheck section for more info.
C
CAF
Caisse d'Allocations Familiales — the department that handles government rent assistance (among other things).
Carte de séjour
A plastic residence card ("visa") received after completing a visa renewal in France. Usually doesn't apply to the assistant visa, more to student visas etc.
Carte Vitale
Medical card that allows you to receive medical treatment at the lowest cost without filing for reimbursement.
Un casier judiciaire
Police record, background check.
CB / carte bleue
Credit card.
Conventionné Secteur 1 (or 2, etc.)
Price levels for doctors. Secteur 1 is the lowest, Secteur 2 more expensive, and so on.
Cuisine
A standard style kitchen. May include an oven, stove, microwave, and fridge.
Une convocation
Notice, summons for an appointment (e.g. convocation de visite médicale for OFII).
D
Un dépôt de garantie
Security deposit.
Doctolib
Website used for making doctor's appointments — see the Medical section for more info.
Dossier
A "file" or "application" — a grouping of forms or documents.
E
Une échéance
Due date, deadline.
Emploi de Temps (E&T)
Schedule. Your school(s) will provide your emploi de temps to tell you which classes you'll teach and when.
En cours de traitement
In processing.
Étudiant(e)
Student. You can only apply for this visa if you are a full-time student in France.
F
Frais de Dossier
Fees dealing with paperwork, or to open an account.
Frais de Gestion
What you pay for someone to manage your account on an ongoing basis.
I
IBAN
International Bank Account Number — your bank account number, located on your RIB.
Un identifiant
Login info, user ID.
J
Un justificatif (de paie, de domicile)
Proof (of payment, of residence).
Justificatif de ressources
Proof of income — can include payslip, tax documents, or bank statements.
L
Une lettre recommandée avec accusé de réception
Letter sent to cancel subscriptions, phone plans, water bills etc.
M
Médecin Traitant
General doctor. Assurance Maladie will send you paperwork to declare your chosen general doctor — this can save you money when they refer you to specialists.
Meilleur Délai
ASAP (as soon as possible).
Un mensualisation
Monthly payment.
Meublé
Furnished. An apartment that comes with essential supplies (furniture, dishes, cooking utensils, bedding). Usually requires only one month's notice to break the lease, but varies by city.
Mutuelle
Added health insurance on top of the sécu.
N
Non-Meublé
Unfurnished. Usually requires three months' notice to break the lease, but varies by city.
P
Par mail
By email (not post).
Par visio(conférence)
By video call.
Une pièce jointe
Attached document (in an email).
Un préavis
Notice, warning — for example, the notice you give your landlord before moving out.
Un prélèvement (automatique)
Autopay for bills.
Une prestation
Benefit, aid (for CAF).
Une prime
Bonus, or money deposited from the government or employer for financial or social aid.
Un procès-verbal (PV)
Statement, signature — you sign one of these when you sign your contract. You should sign a procès-verbal d'installation on your first day of work.
Q
Quittance de loyer
Rent receipt and proof that you've paid your rent.
R
Un rectorat
Board of Education (e.g. le Rectorat de l'académie de Toulouse).
Régler (une facture)
To pay a bill.
Un relevé bancaire
Bank statement.
Des renseignements
Info.
Responsibilité Civile
Liability insurance (often comes with your bank; may be covered by your school) in case you cause damage to a third party. Required in France.
RIB
Relevé d'Identité Bancaire — a document from your bank including your IBAN, bank code, and personal details such as your name.
S
Saisir
To fill in, to enter (info on a form).
Salarié
A different working visa type, for permanent CDI positions. Assistant visas are not salarié visas and follow different rules.
Schengen Zone
A group of European countries with a shared travel agreement (some in the EU, some not). You must leave the Schengen zone to reset your visa — see the FAQs for more.
Scolarité
The admin department of a university or school. You'll receive a certificat de scolarité if enrolled in a master's program, for example.
Sécu
Social Security. Your sécu number is used to apply for your carte vitale and as an identifying number on French paperwork, similar to a social security number in the US. Many assistants never receive a permanent sécu, and some never even get the temporary one.
T
Titre de Séjour
A type of long-stay visa.
Travailleur Temporaire
The visa category for a "standard" assistant. Gives you the right to work in France only for the employer you applied with — not other employers.
T1, T2, T3, T4 (apartments)
T1 = studio, T2 = 1 bedroom + living area, T3 = 2 bedrooms + living area, T4 = 3 bedrooms + living area.
Le traitement (d'un dossier)
Processing of documents.
V
La Vie Scolaire
The school department handling student absences and communication between teachers and students — usually near the entrance, acting as secretaries and hall monitors among other responsibilities. If you can't find your professor, ask the Vie Scolaire for help.
Virement
Transfer, usually related to banks.
