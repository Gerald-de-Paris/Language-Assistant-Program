Application Process

How to Apply

Everything you need to get started, apply, and prepare for interviews — based on official program information and real applicant experience.

Getting Started (Uganda)

  1. Contact Campus France Uganda by email: kampala@campusfrance.org
  2. You'll receive access to the ADELE platform and your login credentials by email
  3. Begin your application on ADELE and upload the required documents. You'll need to appoint a relevant professor as a teacher reference for your application — an interview may also be required
  4. The application deadline is early February

General information: France Éducation International — Becoming a Language Assistant

Register for the ADELE platform here: assistants.france-education-international.fr

The 5 Official Application Stages

This is where many applicants struggle or get disqualified — go through each stage carefully.

Stage 1

Personal Data

  • Personal information, nationality (Ugandan), contact details
  • Select ONE approved institution in your home country (the French Embassy in Uganda) to evaluate your application, then type the message and submit
  • Important: ensure your name matches your passport exactly
Stage 2

Educational Background

  • Upload certificates/diplomas, academic transcripts, and your DELF/DALF certificate
  • Common mistake: uploading incomplete or unclear documents
Stage 3

Destination in France (Regional Choices)

  • Choose three academic regions, one from each region
  • Paris and the Southern region are highly competitive — smaller regions often increase your chances
  • Be flexible
Stage 4

Professeur Évaluateur (Teacher Reference)

  • Moving from this stage can take time — days and delays are normal
  • The PE is listed on the application; don't send multiple invitations at the same time
Stage 5

Strong Points

  • Motivation letter (English & French), work experience, volunteering, projects, command of new technologies, skills and competencies
  • Be honest and specific; avoid relying heavily on ChatGPT
  • Highlight teaching, volunteering, leadership, or cultural projects
  • Show interest in cultural exchange, not just travel

Technical Issues & Traffic

Due to high traffic, you may experience slow loading, difficulty moving to the next stage, or temporary errors. Don't panic. Save frequently, be patient (especially at Stage 4), don't create multiple accounts, and contact Campus France Uganda if you need help.

Interview Process

Once shortlisted, your interview is usually conducted in English and French, to confirm your language level, verify your qualifications and experience, and assess your motivation. The good news: interviews are generally not difficult — basic conversational French is enough. Be calm and honest.

Common Mistakes to Avoid

  • Weak or copied motivation letters
  • Applying late
  • Ignoring French preparation
  • Expecting France or its partners to handle everything
  • Applying without reading instructions
  • Not doing your personal research

Financial Reality

The salary is enough for one person to live on, but France does not cover certain costs upfront. Upon selection, only your visa fee is covered. You'll need to prepare for your flight ticket, first month's expenses, and your accommodation deposit yourself.

€1,600 – €2,500

Recommended budget to cover your flight ticket, first month's expenses, accommodation deposit, and miscellaneous costs before your first paycheck arrives.

Final Advice

  • Start early
  • Prepare your French seriously
  • Follow instructions carefully
  • Be patient with the platform
  • Treat this as a professional opportunity
