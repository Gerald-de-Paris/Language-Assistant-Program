Pre-Arrival · Required Documents

Documents You'll Need

Please read this section before your departure to France. Everything here covers tasks and documents to prepare before you leave.

Passport

Make sure your passport stays valid through the entire time you'll serve as an assistant. If it isn't, start a renewal process immediately. In the meantime, upload your current passport to ADELE and update it with the new one as soon as possible.

Birth Certificate

Locate or request an official copy of your birth certificate — not a photocopy. You need the original or an official copy. Officially recognized European languages generally don't require translation, though at some point during your life in France you may still need an English birth certificate translated (for example, for a driver's license application).

Background Check (Casier Judiciaire)

All assistants need an Identity History Summary / background check from their home country — called different things depending on the country. The French refer to this as un casier judiciaire. Your school needs proof of this to finalize your dossier.

Deadline: June 30
You cannot upload this document to ADELE until you've been placed in your schools and Section 6 becomes available. As of 2024, you must usually upload your birth certificate and background check in Section 6 before receiving your arrête (ADN). Some rectorats send the ADN before these documents are uploaded — if that happens, email the documents directly to your Rectorat.

Some academies may email you requesting a scanned copy — this is normal, just send it by email (par mail). If your academy doesn't specifically request a scanned copy, you only need to upload it into Section 6 of ADELE. Renewing assistants can ask their academy whether last year's Federal Identity History Summary will still be accepted.

How to Get Your Certificate of Good Conduct (Uganda)

  1. Register on the Uganda Police Certificate Services Portal: service.upf.go.ug
  2. Complete the online application with your personal details
  3. Pay the application fee — currently UGX 76,000, plus applicable bank or mobile money charges
  4. Book an appointment for fingerprint capture at your selected Uganda Police INTERPOL office
  5. Attend your appointment with: your original National ID or passport, two passport-size photographs, your printed application form, and proof of payment
  6. After processing (typically about 5-10 working days), collect your Certificate of Good Conduct — generally valid for 6 months

About the ADELE Platform

ADELE is the online platform you'll use to upload your background check and birth certificate. You'll also use it to accept or refuse your assistantship offer, and to receive your work contract (arrêté de nomination / ADN), which you'll need for your visa appointment.

Technical difficulties: email FEI directly at assistant@france-education-international.fr

ADELE FAQs

My arrête has incorrect personal information (name, date of birth, etc.): Contact Campus France Uganda.

I haven't received my arrête yet: It can take until late July, or even late August, to receive your school and placement info. Don't worry — it's coming! Make sure you respond to the survey sent by FEI or TAPIF (USA).

Where can I find my arrête? It will be located in Section 6 of your ADELE account.

To-Do List (June — Before You Receive Your Arrête)

  • Start saving up money
  • Wait for the ADELE email (renewals already have an ADELE account)
  • Renewals: check with your academy whether they'll accept your background check from last year
  • For fingerprints taken in France, you can contact fingerprintsparis@gmail.com
  • New applicants: apply for a federal identity history summary, submit your fingerprints, and upload by June 30
  • Locate your birth certificate — a French translation isn't required as long as it's originally in English
A few general tips: Consider ordering a SIM card that can be shipped to you before you leave. Research your region's climate across the seasons you'll be there so you know what to pack or buy. And start practicing your French — there are plenty of ways to do this before you go.

After You Have Your School Assignment

Once you've received your work contract (arrête), first confirm that you've uploaded all required documents in ADELE (background check, birth certificate, etc.) — academies sometimes send the arrête before this step is complete, so don't skip it.

Next, look up the visa application process for your specific country. Once your visa appointment is secured, reach out to your Professeur Référent (Prof Ref) — your main school contact, who helps with administration and paperwork. Many are also willing to help with things outside school, like your housing search or opening a bank account, though this isn't guaranteed — it doesn't hurt to ask.

Structuring Your First Email to Your Prof Ref

  • Introduce yourself in French, mentioning that this information is also on your ADELE account
  • Use "vous", and check your grammar at least twice before sending
  • Keep the email short — focus on one or two questions rather than everything at once

After that, start researching your city and region — some assistants are placed in smaller towns and commute from a bigger city. Check what transport options are available, and your Prof Ref can help with this too.

Packing List

This is a guide only — not every item will be essential for every assistant.

Clothing

  • Swimwear & towel
  • Sandals & comfortable walking shoes
  • Smart shoes for formal events
  • Winter coat, sweatshirt
  • Casual work clothes (most schools have a relaxed dress code for assistants)
  • Pajamas, underwear, socks
  • Hat, gloves, scarf
  • Jeans, dresses/skirts, t-shirts
  • One nice outfit for special occasions
  • Workout clothes & shoes, belt

Toiletries

  • Shampoo, conditioner, body wash
  • Toothpaste & toothbrush
  • Hairbrush, makeup, perfume
  • Deodorant, sun cream
  • Pads/tampons/cups, face wash

Most of these can be bought after arrival unless you rely on specific brands.

Electronics

  • Laptop & charger
  • Phone & charger
  • Portable charger
  • Headphones (with an aux jack, for the flight)

Caution: hair tools like straighteners often can't handle different voltages and can be a fire hazard. Only bring them if you can confirm dual-voltage support — otherwise buy cheap ones locally.

Paperwork

  • Passport & visa
  • Printed colour copy of your arrête
  • Birth certificate (official copy)
  • ID / driver's licence from home

Miscellaneous

  • Some euros in cash (around €200 if possible) for before your French bank account is set up
  • Sunglasses / sun hat
  • An e-reader if you love books but are limited on luggage space
  • Sports or musical equipment
  • Something from home to share with students/colleagues — check with your Prof Ref before bringing food items, as rules vary by school
Visa process reference: there's a walkthrough video originally made for Kenyan language assistants that may still be useful for Ugandan applicants, since the process is broadly similar: Visa Application Process video. Always confirm the exact steps for Uganda with your embassy, since requirements can differ.
