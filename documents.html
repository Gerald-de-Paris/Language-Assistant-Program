Pre-Arrival · Required Documents

Documents You'll Need

Please read this section before your departure to France. Everything here covers tasks and documents to prepare before you leave.

Passport

Make sure your passport stays valid through the entire time you'll serve as an assistant. If it isn't, start a renewal process immediately. In the meantime, upload your current passport to ADELE and update it with the new one as soon as possible.

Birth Certificate

Locate or request an official copy of your birth certificate — not a photocopy. You need the original or an official copy. Officially recognized European languages generally don't require translation, though at some point during your life in France you may still need an English birth certificate translated (for example, for a driver's license application).

Background Check (Casier Judiciaire)

All assistants need an Identity History Summary / background check from their home country — called different things depending on the country. The French refer to this as un casier judiciaire. Your school needs proof of this to finalize your dossier.

Deadline: June 30
You cannot upload this document to ADELE until you've been placed in your schools and Section 6 becomes available. As of 2024, you must usually upload your birth certificate and background check in Section 6 before receiving your arrête (ADN). Some rectorats send the ADN before these documents are uploaded — if that happens, email the documents directly to your Rectorat.

Some academies may email you requesting a scanned copy — this is normal, just send it by email (par mail). If your academy doesn't specifically request a scanned copy, you only need to upload it into Section 6 of ADELE. Renewing assistants can ask their academy whether last year's Federal Identity History Summary will still be accepted.

How to Get Your Certificate of Good Conduct (Uganda)

  1. Register on the Uganda Police Certificate Services Portal: service.upf.go.ug
  2. Complete the online application with your personal details
  3. Pay the application fee — currently UGX 76,000, plus applicable bank or mobile money charges
  4. Book an appointment for fingerprint capture at your selected Uganda Police INTERPOL office
  5. Attend your appointment with: your original National ID or passport, two passport-size photographs, your printed application form, and proof of payment
  6. After processing (typically about 5 working days), collect your Certificate of Good Conduct — generally valid for 6 months

About the ADELE Platform

ADELE is the online platform you'll use to upload your background check and birth certificate. You'll also use it to accept or refuse your assistantship offer, and to receive your work contract (arrêté de nomination / ADN), which you'll need for your visa appointment.

Technical difficulties: email FEI directly at assistant@france-education-international.fr

ADELE FAQs

My arrête has incorrect personal information (name, date of birth, etc.): Contact Campus France Uganda.

I haven't received my arrête yet: It can take until late July, or even late August, to receive your school and placement info. Don't worry — it's coming! Make sure you respond to the survey sent by FEI or TAPIF (USA).

Where can I find my arrête? It will be located in Section 6 of your ADELE account.
